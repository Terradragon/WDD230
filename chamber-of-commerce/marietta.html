Breif Historical Events

Reported in 1824, Marietta, Georgia started as a small cluster of homes nested near the Cherokee Town of Kennesaw. The state of Georgia was legally recognized ten years later. The city gained some damage during the Civil War when 22 Union agents when they boarded the W & A Railroad train that was set northbound at the Marietta Station. After that, they took over the train until they were later caught by "Andrew Raiders" that ended up being a famous locomotive chase. The recovery after the war was slow for the city, but it eventally began to prosper as new businesses moved in. Finances began to improve, but the burned courthouse left a scar on the city until a new building was constructed in 1872.

Breif Current Events

Today, we have historical sites that still stand as a reminder of the past, and they offer historical information as well as entertainment as visitors come to attend events at Stone Mountain; hike Kennesaw Mountain; and explore the small town of Marietta Square and visit it's many shops and museums. Mareitta is home of some of the following colleges: Kennesaw State University,Life Univeristy, Chattahoochee Technical College, and Lincoln College of Technology.
Some of the sites to see when visiting the city are the following: Gone with the Wind Museum at Historic Brumby Hall; Six Flags White Water; Marietta Museum of History; Mareitta Fire Museum; Mareitta National and Confederate Cemetaries; and Cobb Antique Mall.
